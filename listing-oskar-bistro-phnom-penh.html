The food here was excellent. It had a very nice Riverside view, as well as an inner sanctum that was air conditioned, yet still open to the outside world.

The Buffalo mozzarella cheese pizza with prosciutto was good--And this coming from a Brooklynite born. (Yes, it's a European pizza, but quite good. A nice crispy bottom with minimal flop.)

Thursday night is 30% off wagyu steak night. As you can imagine, wagyu steak is not cheap, beginning with the 200 gram and ending with a 700gram for $99 (This is before the 30% discount.)

I'm thinking that sharing the 700 g wagyu between two people on Thursday night is probably not a bed deal if you're looking to sink your teeth into some red flesh!

They also had a nice arrange of cocktails. None of the above was inexpensive, but it was also quality.

(And they still had a $2 lemon juice for which I am always grateful for upon arriving in Phnom Penh. Fresh lemonade with cane sugar is one of my more favorite items of consumption upon visiting my favorite city in Southeast Asia.)

If you're bargain hunting, or you're a backpack of the less-than-great-smelling variety, You probably want to move on to a more bargain conscientious establishment. However, eating inside with the air conditioning, which is open to riverside quay, is a nice place to have a meal.

Not an inexpensive place, yet a quality establishment.

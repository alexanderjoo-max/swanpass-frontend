That being said, I did notice that the hotel was a bit understaffed, which, to be fair, is an issue happening worldwide in the hospitality industry. My room was on the 4th floor, which is the top floor. This was by far the noisiest hotel experience I've had in a long, long time. I didn't expect this much noise at this price point. It's VERY loud. Four nights of trying to relax and sleep with the heavy streetnoise was unbearable (and I use earplugs and a sleep app on my phone). When I entered my room, I noticed that I had no towels of any kind...and no hand/body soap. The floors in my room were also a bit dirty. The bed linens had multiple stains. The room looked old and worn with previous evidence of water damage on walls and ceiling. The very small shower/tub combo was very unusual and not a great set up and a little awkward to use. The room smelled a bit musty. The hallways also smelled a little damp and moldy. Lots of problems with mosquitoes in my room, too. On the 3rd day of my stay, Bangkok experienced heavy rain. I woke up to find the elevator foyer on the 4th floor with quite a bit of water. There were several buckets collecting water and several ceiling panels removed. The floors were very slippery. My guess is that this is a very common occurrence at the Metropole. Amenities: My reservation included the daily breakfast buffet - the breakfast was fine although quite limited. Not a lot of hot options, unfortunately. The pool is narrow and not very deep. I used it several time and never saw another person in the pool area. The water was a little murky and not as clear I would have liked. What was supposed to be hot/jacuzzi tub in the men's spa area was broken during my stay. The spa showers were nice but soap/shampoo containers were always empty. Location: This hotel is NOT within walking distance of the BTS or MRT. You're at the far end of Soi Thong Lor and need to walk 15 - 20 minutes to the nearest BTS station. You will need a taxi of some kind to get you around. However, there is a water taxi stop just behind the hotel which is a fun, cheap, and convenient way to get around but the water taxi service ends at 7pm. I DO NOT recommend this hotel to other travelers. During my 6 week stay in Asia, this was the only hotel that I was wholeheartedly dissatisfied with. There are better options at this price point...or just spend another $20 - 30/per night and stay at one of the major chain hotels. Good luck!
